How to use map:
The Leuven Places layer is automatically selected on the map. This layer includes all of the images from the Views of Leuven collection. Click on a marker to see the name of the place and the images available. For places with more than one image, click on the left or right arrows to scroll through the images. Sometimes images may take a few moments to load.

To view other layers, hover over the white square in the upper right corner. You can explore the images of the collection by category or century. The categories can be easily identified by different colored markers with icons. The categories include:
religious buildings, public places, university buildings,
panoramic views, parks & waterways, and objects.
The objects layer includes images of items such as business cards, portraits, and reliquaries. It is best to deselect the Leuven Places layer by clicking on the check mark before selecting another layer.

Don’t forget to zoom out — there are a handful of places outside of Leuven as well!

Please note that some markers will not align exactly with where that place is indicated on the old map as there is a degree of error to be expected with old maps.

ABOUT

The Views of Leuven website allows users to explore what Leuven looked like throughout different centuries via an interactive online map. The images are from KU Leuven Libraries' Digital Heritage Online Views of Leuven collection. All of the images in this collection are open data available for reuse. The collection’s metadata is also available as open data via the KU Leuven Libraries GitHub.

This website was created by Allison Bearly, a student of KU Leuven’s Master of Digital Humanities program. This project would not have been possible without the help and guidance of Tjamke Snijders, Nele Gabriëls, Bruno Vandermeulen, Diederik Lanoye, Mark Verbrugge, Fred Truyen, Hans Coppens, Sebastijan Dumancic, the professors of the MDH program, and the rest of the digitization team.
